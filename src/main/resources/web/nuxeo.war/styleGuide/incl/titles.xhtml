Titles

Each page, modal pop up, or widget must contain a title to structure the page. It is important to help users to quickly identify where they are, and to introduce the subject of the page.

h1 title

The h1 title is display in each page of the document management view. Try to always provide one.

h2 title

As an alternative to h1, since the platform is not necessary parsed by google engines, the h2 can be used as a main title with a border.

h3 title

The default h3 title is used as a subtitle, structuring the content with its neutral bottom border.

h3.summaryTitle

This h3, with a .summaryTitle class, is commonly used in all the widgets in the summary view of a document, or in your custom tab design in Studio.

h4 title

A small title with a bottom border, designed to be used to structure small paragraphs.

h5 title

An equivalent of a h4 title, without a border, to structure content included under a h4 title.

h6 title

The smallest title you can use before a paragraph, or in boxes like .foldableBox or .bubbleBox.
